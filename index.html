About Cafe 26

Cafe 26 was originally started by Sheri Lundell, who sold food at the very first Oregon Country Fair (which began as a fundraiser for the freeschool in Eugene). She has been a patron of the festival since its origin, taking a few years off here and there. The booth got its official name some 20-odd years ago when a Highway 26 sign washed into the booth during the annual flooding of the fair grounds.

The booth is family-owned and operated. All of the staff that work the booth are volunteers and considered family.
